✦ Finding existing art can indeed be a great way to save time and find specific styles. Here are some websites that collect game sprites and textures,
  categorized for your convenience:

  Free Resources (Great for Prototyping or Budget-Friendly Development)

   * Kenney.nl: A fantastic resource offering a vast collection of public domain (CC0 licensed) game assets, including 2D sprites, tilesets, and UI packs. All
     assets are free to use, even in commercial projects.
   * OpenGameArt.org: A large community-driven site with a wide variety of freely licensed art, including 2D sprites, tilesets, and backgrounds. You'll find a
     mix of styles and quality, but it's a treasure trove for free assets.
   * itch.io (Assets Section): While primarily a marketplace for indie games, itch.io also hosts thousands of free sprite packs, tilesets, and UI elements.
     Many creators offer their assets for free or "pay what you want."
   * CraftPix.net (Free Section): Offers a selection of free 2D game assets, including characters, tilesets, and backgrounds, alongside their premium
     offerings.
   * Game Art 2D (Free Section): Similar to CraftPix, they provide a collection of free 2D assets like platformer tilesets and character spritesheets.

  Paid Marketplaces / High-Quality Assets (For More Specific Styles and Production-Ready Art)

   * CraftPix.net: Beyond their free section, CraftPix offers high-quality premium 2D game assets, including detailed characters, icons, tilesets, and GUI
     elements.
   * Game Art 2D: Provides royalty-free 2D game assets, including platformer and top-down tilesets, character spritesheets, and backgrounds, often with a
     consistent art style across packs.
   * Unity Asset Store (2D Section): Even if you're not using Unity, many 2D assets here are provided as standard image files (like PNGs) and can be used in
     other game engines. It's a massive marketplace with a wide range of styles and quality.
   * GameDev Market: A marketplace for high-quality, affordable 2D, 3D, GUI, and audio game assets. You can find unique art styles here.

  When browsing these sites, pay close attention to the licensing terms for each asset, especially if you plan to use them in a commercial game.
